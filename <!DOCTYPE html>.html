About Us

Giving Back Foundation is a national, humanitarian, non-governmental, non- profit making and non-partisan organization registered by Rehabilitation and relief commission with registration No. 4927.
The foundation is dedicated to saving lives and commits to making positive impact and addressing the most urgent and pressing needs of vulnerable children, adults and communities in South Sudan through education and health programs. Our sole mission is simple.
To serve the vulnerable communities, to fight vulnerability, relief suffering and provide necessary tools and assistance to move them towards self-reliance and better future. By offering educational and medical services and related activities to the people at the highest risk such as orphans, street children, widows and vulnerable adults, GBF rehabilitates their lives and restore lost hope.
GBF mainstream and integrates cross-cutting programs such as environmental protection and disability support. The foundation also integrates and applies the principle of solidarity and compassion, Be the light in the world of darkness and taking the responsibility to "give back" to the communities. For years, fulfilling our commitment to community services of serving the neglected and abandoned children and adults has given us sense of purpose. Our aspiration is to be the light in the darkness and a place of solace for those in need.

Our thematic areas of focus are;

Health-health education campaigns, mental health support and free medical services for the orphans, street children and vulnerable adults

Education-Educating the most vulnerable children and advocating for their rights.

Tackling climate change through environmental protection such as trees plantation.

CORE VALUES

Voluntary Service:

We voluntarily give our efforts to serve and recuse. The movement is a voluntarily relief Movement not prompted in any manners by desire of gain.

Accountability:

We are answerable for our actions.

Compassion:

We are sympathetic, pity and concern for the suffering and so we desire to work and act with compassion.

Community:

We desire to work together with local churches and local communities with a spirit of humanity and accountability, impacting communities by helping the most vulnerable.

Integrity:

We desire to be transparent in all our labor and with all resources entrusted to us. We build trust each and everyday by conducting ourselves in a way that enables others to trust our work with confidence.

Neutrality:

We consider every community as one regardless of their race, religious and ethnicity.

Courage:

We take action and make it happens (action is a measure of our progress and strength).

Humanity:

Our heart is to help, care and changes lives, suffering is universal and require a response. It cannot be met with indifference.

What We Do

1. Conducting medical outreaches to vulnerable children and remote communities.

2. Provision of health education and hygiene promotion to vulnerable children and adults.

3. Psychological and Mental health support and awareness to traumatized children and vulnerable adults.

4. Advocate and campaign for vulnerable children rights and protect their interest in South Sudan.

5. Supporting underprivileged and most vulnerable children from surviving to thriving by educating them.

6. Fighting climate change through environmental protection such as trees plantation and

advocacy for betier environment.

7. Provision of disability aids such as wheelchairs, crunches & canes and Socio- economic empowerment to eradicate household poverty among the victims of arms conflicts.

OUR RECENT ACTIVITIES

Our recent actvites involve Medical outreach to the Stone Church Orphanage. This charity work led to the following results:

1. Treatment of 231 orphaned children with various medical conditions.

2. Distribution of 180 sanitary items to the adolescence girls at the orphanage.

3. Provision of female Hygiene education and motivational talk.

4. Provision of health tips to the children and staffs at orphanage

The community's response to the Giving Back Foundation

Overall, the community's response is likely to be one of admiration, support, and encouragement for the Giving Back Foundation's meaningful contributions to the well-being of vulnerable individuals in South Sudan.
